# GRA in Painting

## Colour and Composition

Visual elements as hierarchical foam:
- $k=0$: Pixels, brushstrokes
- $k=1$: Shapes, local colour relationships
- $k=2$: Composition, balance, rhythm
- $k=3$: Symbolism, narrative, emotional impact

**Foam**: Clashing colours, compositional imbalance, thematic contradictions
**Nullification**: Harmonisation, focal point establishment, thematic coherence

## Generative Art

Use GRA to evolve paintings:
1. Initialise random canvas (high foam)
2. Apply nullification operators (brushstroke rules)
3. Measure visual $\Phi$ via edge detection, colour histogram entropy
4. Iterate until stability or intentional instability

## Art History

Analyse masterpieces through GRA lens:
- Renaissance: High $R_{	ext{hier}}$ (deep symbolic hierarchy)
- Impressionism: Low $R_{	ext{hier}}$, wave-like (light as primary)
- Cubism: Foam explosion (multiple viewpoints)
- Minimalism: Aggressive nullification (maximum reduction)
